Week 1: Understanding AI Fundamentals
Build your conceptual foundation
Day 1-2: What is AI, ML, and Deep Learning?
Watch 3Blue1Brown's "But what is a neural network?" video. Read Andrew Ng's intro to AI. Write a one-page summary in your own words.
Day 3: Explore Real-World AI Applications
Research 5 AI use cases across different industries (healthcare, finance, entertainment, etc.). Create a mind map of how AI is being used today.
Day 4-5: Master Prompt Engineering
Spend 2 hours practicing with ChatGPT/Claude. Learn techniques: be specific, use examples, iterate, chain prompts. Document 10 effective prompts you created.
Day 6: AI Ethics & Limitations
Read about AI bias, hallucinations, and ethical concerns. Write reflections on responsible AI use.
Day 7: Week 1 Review & Project
Create a presentation or blog post explaining AI to a non-technical friend. This consolidates your learning.
Week 2: Mastering AI Tools
Become power user of AI assistants
Day 8-9: Advanced ChatGPT/Claude Techniques
Learn: custom instructions, code interpreter, document analysis, multimodal capabilities. Complete 5 real tasks using these features.
Day 10: Explore Specialized AI Tools
Try 3 tools: Midjourney/DALL-E (images), ElevenLabs (voice), Runway (video). Create one output with each.
Day 11-12: AI for Productivity
Learn to use AI for: email writing, research, summarization, brainstorming. Create templates for your common tasks.
Day 13: AI APIs & Integration
Read OpenAI and Anthropic API documentation. Understand concepts: tokens, temperature, system prompts. No coding yetâ€”just understand the concepts.
Day 14: Week 2 Mini-Project
Use AI to solve a real problem in your life: automate a workflow, research a topic, create content. Document the process.
Week 3: Machine Learning Fundamentals
Understanding how AI learns
Day 15-16: Supervised vs Unsupervised Learning
Learn the difference between supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning. Study examples: classification, regression, clustering.
Day 17: Training Data & Models
Understand: training data, validation, testing, overfitting, underfitting. Watch StatQuest videos on these topics.
Day 18-19: Neural Networks Deep Dive
Learn architecture: layers, neurons, weights, biases, activation functions. Use TensorFlow Playground to experiment visually.
Day 20: Large Language Models (LLMs)
Study how ChatGPT works: transformers, attention mechanism, pre-training vs fine-tuning. Read Jay Alammar's illustrated guides.
Day 21: Week 3 Knowledge Check
Create flashcards for all ML concepts. Teach someone (or write as if teaching) about how neural networks learn.
Week 4: Building AI Applications
From theory to practice
Day 22-23: Python Basics for AI
If new to Python: learn variables, functions, lists, dictionaries. Complete Python tutorial on Codecademy or DataCamp (3-4 hours).
Day 24-25: First AI API Project
Build a simple chatbot using OpenAI or Anthropic API. Follow official quickstart guides. Get it working end-to-end.
Day 26: Experiment with Prompts Programmatically
Modify your chatbot to test different system prompts, temperatures, and parameters. Document what works best for different tasks.
Day 27-28: Build Something Useful
Create an AI tool that solves a real problem: document summarizer, email classifier, Q&A system, content generator, etc.
Day 29: Learn About Fine-tuning & RAG
Research: when to fine-tune vs use RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation). Understand vector databases and embeddings conceptually.
Day 30: Final Project & Next Steps
Polish your Week 4 project. Share it online (GitHub, blog, LinkedIn). Create your 60-day learning plan to continue the journey.
